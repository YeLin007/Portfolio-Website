AR Name Card

Unity Vuforia 3D Interaction

This AR Name Card project turns a simple printed card into an interactive experience. When the user scans the card with their phone, a 3D logo appears on top, together with my name and role. The goal was to make a memorable “first impression” using augmented reality.

Watch AR Demo

Demo video shows the AR Name Card in action on an Android phone.

Overview

This project was part of my Immersive Technology module. We were asked to design a physical name card and extend it with an AR layer using Unity and Vuforia. When the camera detects the card as an image target, a 3D object and text information appear on top.

I designed the card layout first, then created a simple 3D logo/avatar and integrated it in Unity so it matches the printed design and appears at the right scale and position.

Project Details

  • Role: AR designer & developer
  • Tools: Unity, Vuforia, Illustrator
  • Target Platform: Mobile AR (Android)
  • Focus: Image targets, 3D placement, user experience

Design & Setup

From flat name card to interactive AR marker.

01 · Name Card Layout

I started by designing a clean name card with my logo, name, role, and contact details. I kept strong contrast so that Vuforia could track the image reliably.

02 · Image Target in Vuforia

The front of the card was uploaded to Vuforia as an image target. After getting a good star rating, I imported the database into Unity and set up the ARCamera.

03 · 3D Logo & Text

I created a simple 3D logo/avatar and positioned it on top of the name card target. Extra text objects show my name and role, giving more context when viewed in AR.

04 · Build & Testing

Finally, I built the project to Android and tested it with my printed card. I adjusted scale, lighting, and distance so the AR content feels stable and readable.

Key Interactions

Simple but meaningful AR behaviours.

Scan & Reveal

When the camera detects the card, the 3D logo pops up smoothly. This makes the physical card feel “alive” instead of just static paper.

Hover Information

Additional floating text shows my name, role, and a short tagline. This provides extra context without needing more space on the printed card.

Call-to-Action Ideas

In future iterations, I can add buttons or icons in AR that link to my portfolio, LinkedIn, or email, turning the name card into a small interactive menu.

What I Learned

This project helped me understand how digital content can extend a physical object. I learned:

  • How to set up ARCamera and image targets using Unity and Vuforia.
  • Why good contrast and clear shapes are important for image tracking.
  • How to position and scale 3D objects so they line up with real-world markers.

Next Steps

  • Add animated transitions when the logo appears or disappears.
  • Include interactive buttons that open portfolio or contact links.
  • Explore using multiple targets (e.g., front and back of the card).
